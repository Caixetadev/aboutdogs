History of dogs

The dog (scientific name: Canis lupus familiaris), in the United States also called dog, is a carnivorous mammal of the family of canids, subspecies of the wolf, and perhaps the oldest animal domesticated by humans. Theories postulate that it emerged from the gray wolf on the Asian continent more than 100,000 years ago.
